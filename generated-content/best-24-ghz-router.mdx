## What to Look for Before Buying the Best 24 Ghz Router

### Find Your Need - Compatibility and use case considerations
Before purchasing, identify how many devices you currently own and how many you plan to add. If you live in a small apartment with only a few devices, a budget-friendly AC1200 model may be sufficient. However, if you have a smart home with dozens of IoT devices, look for a router that supports OFDMA and higher device capacities. Ensure the router is compatible with your current Internet Service Provider (ISP) and modem.

### Budget - Setting realistic price expectations ($X-$Y ranges)
Setting a budget is crucial. Entry-level routers typically range from $40 to $80 and are great for basic needs. Mid-range WiFi 6 routers usually sit between $90 and $180, offering a significant performance boost. High-end WiFi 6E or WiFi 7 routers can range from $200 to over $500. While expensive, these top-tier models provide future-proofing and extreme speeds for professionals and gamers.

### Key Features - Most important technical specifications
Focus on the bands and ports. Dual-band (2.4GHz and 5GHz) is standard, but Tri-band (adding 6GHz) is better for crowded areas. Check for models with robust thermal management. Routers can generate significant heat during heavy use, which can lead to throttling. Look for large heat sinks or well-ventilated case designs. High-gain external antennas are generally more durable and provide better signal directionality than internal ones.
