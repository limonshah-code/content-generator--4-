## What to Look for Before Buying the Best Vacuum Robot For Pet Hair

### 1. Find Your Need - Compatibility and use case considerations
Before making a purchase, assess the ratio of hard floors to carpets in your home. If you have thick carpets, prioritize high suction power (above 5000 Pa) and a specialized brushroll. If you have mostly hard floors, a unit with a sonic mopping feature will be more beneficial for removing muddy paw prints and fine dander.

### 2. Budget - Setting realistic price expectations ($X-$Y ranges)
- **$200-$400:** You can find reliable suction and basic smart mapping, often without a self-empty base or with a smaller capacity one.
- **$400-$700:** This is the sweet spot where you get advanced LiDAR mapping, self-emptying bases with 60-90 day capacity, and integrated mopping.
- **$700+:** Premium models offer extreme suction (10,000 Pa+), sonic mopping, and sophisticated obstacle avoidance.

### 3. Key Features - Most important technical specifications
Look for LiDAR (Laser) navigation over camera-based systems as it works better in the dark. For pet owners, an anti-hair wrap or self-cleaning brushroll is the most critical mechanical feature. This prevents you from having to manually cut hair out of the brush every week.

### 4. Quality & Durability - What to look for in construction
Check for reinforced wheels and sensors. Robots that have a modular design where brushes and filters are easily accessible for replacement tend to last longer. A good warranty, like the two-year coverage found on some models, is a strong indicator of build confidence.

### 5. Brand Reputation - Importance of established manufacturers
Brands like Roborock, Shark, and iRobot have a history of frequent software updates and readily available replacement parts. Choosing a well-known brand ensures that you can find filters and bags easily for years to come.

### 6. Portability/Size - Considerations for space and storage
Measure the clearance under your lowest furniture. If you have a low-profile couch, look for a robot thinner than 3 inches. Also, consider the size of the self-empty dock; some are quite tall and require a specific amount of clearance to function properly.

Choosing the right unit requires a step-by-step approach to ensure you do not overspend on features you do not need or underspend on a weak motor. Start by identifying the hair length of your pets.
