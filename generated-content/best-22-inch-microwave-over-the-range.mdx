## What to Look for Before Buying the Best 22 Inch Microwave Over The Range

### 1. Find Your Need - Compatibility and use case considerations
Before purchasing, measure your available space carefully. While these are often referred to as 22-inch or 30-inch units, the internal and external dimensions vary slightly. Consider if you need a high-powered vent for heavy stovetop frying or if you primarily use the microwave for simple reheating and defrosting.

### 2. Budget - Setting realistic price expectations ($200-$600 ranges)
Basic over-the-range microwaves usually start around $200-$300. If you are looking for advanced features like air frying, convection baking, or smart home connectivity, expect to pay between $400 and $600. Setting a budget early helps narrow down whether you want a standard workhorse or a multi-functional kitchen tool.

### 3. Key Features - Most important technical specifications
Look for a wattage of at least 1000W for efficient cooking. Ventilation power is measured in CFM (Cubic Feet per Minute); a rating of 300 CFM is standard, but 400 CFM is better if you cook with a lot of oil or spices. Sensor cooking is another vital feature that prevents overcooking by detecting steam.

### 4. Quality & Durability - What to look for in construction
Check the quality of the door hinges and the responsiveness of the control panel. Stainless steel is durable and heat-resistant, but some modern plastics used in white or black models are also very sturdy. Look for brands that offer extended warranties as a sign of build quality.

### 5. Brand Reputation - Importance of established manufacturers
Established brands like GE, BLACK+DECKER, and Frigidaire have extensive service networks. This is important for over-the-range units because they are semi-permanent installations. Choosing a well-known brand ensures you can find replacement parts or professional repair services if needed years down the line.

### 6. Portability/Size - Considerations for space and storage
Over-the-range units are designed to save counter space. If you have low-hanging cabinets, look specifically for slim or low-profile models. Ensure the unit you choose allows for the type of venting your kitchen uses, whether it is ducted to the outside or recirculating via a charcoal filter.

## Is it A Wise Decision to Buy 22 inch microwave over the range?

Investing in an over-the-range microwave is one of the most effective ways to upgrade a kitchen. By moving the microwave off the counter and above the stove, you immediately reclaim several square feet of workspace, which is a major benefit in smaller homes or apartments. 

Beyond space savings, these units provide dual functionality by acting as both a microwave and a range hood. This eliminates the need for a separate ventilation fan, saving you money on appliances and installation. Modern units also offer features like air frying and convection baking, meaning one appliance can do the work of three. 

While the initial cost and installation are higher than a countertop model, the long-term value in kitchen efficiency and home resale value makes it a wise decision for most homeowners. The durability of these units, combined with the convenience of eye-level cooking, provides a superior experience that countertop models simply cannot match.

## What Is the Best Way to Choose 22 inch microwave over the range?

Choosing the best unit starts with a precise measurement of your cabinet opening. Most standard units are 30 inches wide, but the term 22 inch often refers to specific compact depths or internal sizes. Once you have your dimensions, follow these steps:

1. Determine your venting needs: Does your kitchen vent to the outside, or do you need a recirculating (ductless) model?
2. Choose your must-have features: Do you want an air fryer built-in, or is a standard microwave enough?
3. Check the CFM rating: If you sear meat or fry often, aim for 400 CFM.
4. Read recent user reviews: Look for comments on noise levels and ease of installation.
5. Verify the warranty: A longer warranty usually indicates a more reliable motor and magnetron.

Avoid the common mistake of buying based on price alone; a cheap unit with a weak fan will leave your kitchen smelling like old grease. If your budget allows, upgrading to a sensor-cook model will save you from thousands of instances of overcooked or cold-centered food over the life of the appliance.
