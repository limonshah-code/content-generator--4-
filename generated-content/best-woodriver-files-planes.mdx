## What to Look for Before Buying the Best Woodriver Files Planes

### Find Your Need
Before investing in a hand tool, identify what stage of woodworking you are in. If you are just starting, a versatile tool like the #5 Jack Plane is a great first choice because it can do a bit of everything. However, if you are struggling with end grain, the Low Angle Block Plane should be your priority. Understanding whether you need to remove a lot of material quickly or achieve a final, mirror-like finish will dictate which plane or file is right for your shop.

### Budget
WoodRiver tools occupy a sweet spot in the market. They are more expensive than entry-level tools but significantly more affordable than boutique hand-made options. Expect to spend between $40 for a good set of files and $350 for a large jointer plane. Setting a realistic budget involves considering the longevity of the tool; these are heirloom-quality items that will last decades with proper care, making the initial investment more palatable over time.

### Key Features
Look for the Bedrock-style frog in bench planes, as this allows for easier adjustments without disassembly. For files, double-cut teeth are essential for efficient material removal. High carbon steel blades are a must for edge retention, and Bubinga handles provide a superior tactile experience compared to synthetic alternatives. Always check for the V3 designation on WoodRiver planes, as this indicates the most refined version of their manufacturing process.

### Quality & Durability
The materials used in construction are the best indicators of durability. Ductile iron is preferred for plane bodies because it is less brittle than grey iron and resists warping. Machined surfaces should be flat and square within 0.003 inches or less. For files, the teeth should be uniformly sharp and the handles securely attached. A high-quality tool should feel heavy and solid in the hand, with no play in the adjustment mechanisms.

### Brand Reputation
WoodRiver is a brand owned by Woodcraft, a staple in the American woodworking community. Their reputation is built on providing professional-grade tools to the masses. Choosing a reputable brand ensures that you have access to replacement parts, like blades and screws, and that the manufacturer stands behind their product. It also means there is a wealth of community knowledge and tutorials available online for tuning and using these specific tools.

### Portability/Size
Consider the physical layout of your shop. A 22-inch jointer plane requires a large bench and significant storage space. If you work in a small apartment or a compact garage, smaller tools like the #4 smoother or a set of files might be more practical. Always ensure you have a safe, dry place to store these tools to prevent rust and damage to the precision-ground surfaces.
