## What to Look for Before Buying the Best Cleaner For Trex Decking

### Find Your Need - Compatibility and use case considerations
Before you purchase a cleaner, you must understand your deck's specific needs. Are you dealing with green algae in a shaded area, or black mold spots that have appeared over time? Some cleaners are designed for broad maintenance, while others, like Wash Safe, are specifically for organic stains. Additionally, check your deck's warranty. Some manufacturers, like TimberTech or Trex, may recommend specific formulas to avoid voiding your long-term protection.

### Budget - Setting realistic price expectations ($15-$60 ranges)
Cleaning solutions vary widely in price based on their concentration and specialized features. A simple 32-ounce spray for spot cleaning might cost around $15 to $20. For full-deck restoration, concentrated gallons that make multiple gallons of solution are more cost-effective, typically ranging from $30 to $60. Consider the square footage of your deck to calculate the cost per square foot; often, the more expensive gallon of concentrate is actually the cheaper option long-term.

### Key Features - Most important technical specifications
Look for surfactants, which are the ingredients that break the surface tension and lift dirt out of the textured pores of the composite. UV protection is another high-value feature found in premium brands like STAR BRITE, which helps prevent the plastic components of the deck from fading in the sun. If you want to avoid manual labor, look for no-scrub or spray-and-forget formulas, though these often take longer to show final results.
