## What to Look for Before Buying the Best Antique Throw Pillow Covers

### 1. Find Your Need - Compatibility and Use Case
Before purchasing, consider where the pillows will live. If they are for a high-traffic sofa, you need durable materials like chenille or thick linen. For a decorative bed accent, luxurious velvet or silk-like jacquard might be better. Always measure your existing inserts; a common trick is to buy a cover 1-2 inches smaller than your insert for a plump, high-end look.

### 2. Budget - Setting Realistic Price Expectations
Antique-style covers generally range from $10 to $40 per pair.
- **$10-$15:** Single-sided prints, basic cotton/polyester blends.
- **$15-$25:** Double-sided prints, soft velvet or linen, sets of two.
- **$25-$40:** High-end jacquard, hand-sewn tassels, or premium heavy fabrics.

### 3. Key Features - Technical Specifications
Look for an invisible or hidden zipper. This prevents the hardware from scratching your furniture and provides a cleaner look. Double-sided printing is another major feature to seek out, as it ensures the pillow looks great regardless of how it is tossed onto the sofa.

### 4. Quality & Durability - Construction Essentials
Check the seam strength and the type of fabric. Chenille and velvet are great for softness, while linen offers breathability. Ensure the edges are finished properly with overlock stitching to prevent fraying, especially if you plan on machine washing them frequently.

### 5. Brand Reputation
Brands like Loom and Mill or Yangest have established a reputation for consistent sizing and color accuracy. While many boutique brands offer great designs, checking user reviews for 'color bleeding' or 'zipper failure' is essential before committing to a purchase.

### 6. Portability/Size - Storage Considerations
Standard sizes are 18x18 and 20x20. Lumbar sizes (12x20) are excellent for providing back support. Since these are covers only, they are extremely easy to store in a linen closet or drawer when you want to change your seasonal decor, making them a space-saving way to refresh your home.

## Is it A Wise Decision to Buy antique throw pillow covers?

Investing in antique throw pillow covers is one of the most cost-effective ways to change the entire atmosphere of a room. Instead of buying new furniture or expensive rugs, a set of well-chosen covers can introduce a sense of history and sophistication for a fraction of the cost. These covers allow you to experiment with colors like bronze, coffee brown, and dark blue without a permanent commitment.

Furthermore, these covers protect your actual pillow inserts from dust, oils, and wear. Most modern antique-style covers are made with durable, machine-washable fabrics that far outperform actual vintage textiles in terms of maintenance. This makes them a wise choice for busy households that want a high-end look without the fragile nature of true antiques.

## What Is the Best Way to Choose antique throw pillow covers?

Selecting the right cover starts with your existing color palette. Look for a cover that shares at least one color with your sofa or rug to create a cohesive look. 

1. **Step 1:** Measure your pillow inserts. If you want a full look, choose a cover that is slightly smaller than the insert.
2. **Step 2:** Choose your texture. Velvet for luxury, linen for a rustic feel, or chenille for cozy farmhouse vibes.
3. **Step 3:** Decide on a pattern. If your sofa is solid-colored, go for a bold Tabriz or Turkish block print. If your furniture has a pattern, choose a more subtle floral or solid-textured cover.
4. **Step 4:** Check the care instructions. If you have pets or kids, ensure the covers are machine washable.
5. **Step 5:** Read reviews regarding color accuracy, as digital screens can sometimes misrepresent the deep tones of antique styles.

## FAQ

**What is the best antique throw pillow covers for beginners?**
For those just starting, we recommend the Yangest 18x18 Chenille covers. Their neutral coffee brown color and soft texture make them incredibly easy to style in almost any room. They are durable enough to handle daily use while providing a significant upgrade in aesthetic appeal without being too overwhelming in pattern or color.

**How much should I spend on antique throw pillow covers?**
A budget of $20 to $30 is usually the sweet spot. This range typically gets you a set of two high-quality covers with double-sided printing and a hidden zipper. While cheaper options exist, they often feature single-sided prints and thinner materials that may not last as long or look as premium.

**What features are most important in antique throw pillow covers?**
The most important features are the quality of the zipper, the fabric weight, and whether the pattern is printed on both sides. A sturdy, invisible zipper is crucial for durability, while a heavier fabric like jacquard or velvet ensures the pillow maintains its shape and looks high-end on your couch.

**How do I maintain my antique throw pillow covers?**
Most modern antique-style covers are machine washable. You should wash them inside out on a gentle cycle with cold water to preserve the colors. Avoid using bleach, and it is usually best to air dry them or use a low-heat tumble setting to prevent the fabric from shrinking or losing its soft texture.

**Are expensive antique throw pillow covers worth it?**
Yes, if you are looking for specific details like hand-applied tassels, gold piping, or genuine jacquard weaving. These premium features add a level of authenticity and luxury that basic printed covers cannot match. If the pillow is a focal point of your room, investing a bit more in a brand like Loom and Mill is worthwhile.

**What brands make the best antique throw pillow covers?**
Brands like Yangest, Loom and Mill, and Kawani are currently leading the market. They offer a great balance of historical designs and modern fabric technology. They are known for their consistent quality, accurate color representation, and use of durable zippers that do not break easily during use.

**How long do antique throw pillow covers typically last?**
If cared for properly, high-quality covers made from linen, velvet, or polyester can last 3 to 5 years of regular use. To extend their life, rotate them occasionally and follow the specific washing instructions provided by the manufacturer. High-quality prints are designed not to fade quickly, even with regular washing.

**Can I find good antique throw pillow covers on a budget?**
Absolutely. Sets from brands like Kawani and Sweetshow provide two covers for under $20. These budget options often use cotton linen or microfiber to keep costs down while still offering beautiful, clear prints that mimic the look of expensive antique rugs and tapestries.
