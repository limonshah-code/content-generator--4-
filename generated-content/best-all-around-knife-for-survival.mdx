## What to Look for Before Buying the Best All Around Knife For Survival

### Find Your Need - Compatibility and use case considerations
Before you purchase, you must identify your primary environment. If you are in thick jungle, a large bowie like the FLISSA or Mossy Oak is essential for clearing paths. For standard camping or bushcraft, a 4 to 5-inch blade like the Gerber Ultimate or SOG Field Knife offers better control for carving and food prep. Consider whether you need a folding knife for daily convenience or a fixed blade for survival-level strength.

### Budget - Setting realistic price expectations ($X-$Y ranges)
You can find reliable survival knives across various price points. Budget options ($20–$40) like the NedFoss or CRKT offer great value but might use standard stainless steel. Mid-range choices ($40–$70) like the Gerber or SOG often include better handle materials and versatile sheaths. Premium options ($70+) typically feature D2 or other high-carbon steels that hold edges longer and offer artisan craftsmanship like the DRGSKL Japanese Tanto.

### Key Features - Most important technical specifications
Look for a full tang design, where the steel of the blade runs the entire length of the handle, as this prevents the knife from breaking during prying or chopping. A "drop point" or "clip point" is generally best for all-around utility. Additionally, integrated features like a fire starter, sharpener, or emergency whistle can save space in your pack and provide redundancy in your survival kit.

### Quality & Durability - What to look for in construction
Pay attention to the steel type. Stainless steel (like 7Cr17MoV) is great for rust resistance in wet climates, while High Carbon or D2 steel is preferred for long-term sharpness. Handle materials like G10, Micarta, or high-quality rubber provide the best grip. Avoid plastic handles that can become slippery or brittle over time. A good Kydex or heavy-duty nylon sheath is also vital for protecting both the blade and the user.

### Brand Reputation - Importance of established manufacturers
Buying from established brands like Gerber, SOG, or CRKT ensures you are getting a product backed by years of field testing and professional design. These companies often offer lifetime warranties and have better quality control. However, newer brands like NedFoss and FLISSA are making waves by offering high-end features at lower prices, provided you check user reviews for consistency.

### Portability/Size - Considerations for space and storage
A 15-inch knife might look impressive, but it can be cumbersome if you are hiking 20 miles a day. Conversely, a 2-inch neck knife might be too small for heavy tasks. Think about how you will carry the tool—belt, neck, or inside a pack. Ensure the sheath has a secure mounting system that matches your gear, such as MOLLE compatibility or a reliable belt loop.

## Is it A Wise Decision to Buy all around knife for survival?

Investing in a high-quality survival knife is one of the smartest decisions any outdoor enthusiast can make. Unlike cheaper alternatives, a dedicated survival knife is built to withstand extreme forces—chopping, prying, and batoning—that would shatter a standard kitchen or pocket knife. When you are in the wilderness, your knife is your most important tool for shelter building, fire starting, and food processing. 

Compared to multi-tools, which have many small parts that can break, a fixed-blade survival knife is a single, solid piece of engineering that offers unmatched reliability. While the initial cost might be higher, the long-term benefit is a tool that can last a lifetime with proper care. It provides a level of safety and self-reliance that simply cannot be quantified in dollars. For the price of a few restaurant meals, you gain a life-saving instrument that ensures you are prepared for the unexpected.

## What Is the Best Way to Choose all around knife for survival?

Selecting the right blade requires a systematic approach to ensure it fits your hand and your mission. Follow these steps:

1.  **Define the Primary Task:** Will you be chopping wood (get a heavy fixed blade) or performing fine carving (get a smaller, ergonomic blade)?
2.  **Choose Your Steel:** If you live near salt water, prioritize stainless steel. If you want a razor-sharp edge for months, look for D2 or High Carbon steel.
3.  **Test the Grip:** If possible, choose a handle material like G10 or TPR that feels secure even when your hands are wet.
4.  **Evaluate the Sheath:** A knife is only useful if it is easy to carry. Look for Kydex for a modern, secure fit or nylon for lightweight flexibility.
5.  **Look for Redundancy:** Choosing a knife with a built-in fire starter or sharpener adds a layer of security to your kit without adding bulk.
6.  **Avoid Common Mistakes:** Do not choose a knife based solely on looks or a "rambo" aesthetic. Functionality and comfort should always come first.
7.  **Check the Tang:** Always verify that a fixed blade is "full tang" for any serious survival work.

## FAQ

**What is the best all around knife for survival for beginners?**
For beginners, the Gerber Ultimate Survival Knife is the best choice. It includes all the necessary survival extras like a fire starter and whistle, and its half-serrated blade is very forgiving for various tasks. It provides a complete "survival system" in one package, making it easy for newcomers to feel prepared without needing to buy multiple separate tools.

**How much should I spend on all around knife for survival?**
A budget of $40 to $75 is the sweet spot for a high-quality, reliable survival knife. In this range, you get durable materials like stainless steel or D2 and well-designed sheaths. While you can spend $200+, most users will find that a $60 tool from a reputable brand like SOG or Gerber handles 99% of survival tasks perfectly.

**What features are most important in all around knife for survival?**
The three most critical features are a full tang construction for strength, a comfortable non-slip handle for safety, and a high-quality sheath for secure carry. Secondary features like a fire starter or an integrated sharpener are very helpful but should not come at the expense of a solid, well-made blade.

**How do I maintain my all around knife for survival?**
Keep the blade clean and dry after every use. For high-carbon or D2 steel knives, apply a thin coat of mineral oil to prevent rust. Sharpen the edge regularly using the integrated sharpener or a whetstone. Occasionally check the sheath for debris and ensure the handle screws (if any) remain tight and secure.

**Are expensive all around knife for survival worth it?**
Expensive knives are worth it if they use premium steels like D2 or S30V, which offer much better edge retention and toughness. However, for the average hiker, a mid-range knife is usually sufficient. You are paying for the quality of the heat treatment and the durability of the handle materials, which can be critical in long-term survival scenarios.

**What brands make the best all around knife for survival?**
Gerber, SOG, CRKT, and Mossy Oak are all highly respected for their survival lines. Brands like FLISSA and NedFoss are also excellent for those looking for great features at a lower price point. Always look for brands that offer a warranty, as it shows they stand behind the durability of their products.

**How long do all around knife for survival typically last?**
A high-quality survival knife should last a lifetime if maintained properly. The steel can be sharpened thousands of times, and materials like G10 or rubber are designed to withstand decades of exposure to the elements. Only extreme abuse, such as using the knife as a pry bar beyond its limits, should cause it to fail.

**Can I find good all around knife for survival on a budget?**
Yes, the Mossy Oak and FLISSA bowie knives are excellent examples of budget-friendly tools that do not sacrifice the essential full-tang strength. While the steel might require more frequent sharpening than premium D2, they are more than capable of handling any survival situation you might encounter in the wild.
