## What to Look for Before Buying the Best Cheap Stainless Steel Microwave

### 1. Find Your Need - Compatibility and use case considerations
Before you hit the buy button, consider how you actually use your microwave. If you only use it for reheating coffee or small bowls of soup, a 0.7 cu ft model with 700W of power is perfectly fine. However, if you plan to defrost large cuts of meat or cook whole frozen meals, you should look for at least 1000W and a 1.1 cu ft capacity or higher. Check your counter space carefully, as some high-power models require significant clearance for venting.

### 2. Budget - Setting realistic price expectations ($80-$180)
For the cheap stainless steel microwave category, you can expect to spend between $80 and $180. The $80 to $110 range usually covers basic 700W to 900W models with simple digital controls. Once you cross the $130 mark, you start seeing advanced features like sensor cooking, convection modes, and air frying capabilities. Investing a little more upfront for a 1000W+ unit can save you time and provide a more even cooking experience.

### 3. Key Features - Most important technical specifications
Look for features that enhance usability. A 30-second express button is a must-have for quick tasks. If you live in a small space, a Mute Function is invaluable for keeping the house quiet. Child safety locks are essential for families. Additionally, check the turntable size; a larger turntable allows for larger plates and helps prevent food from hitting the side walls, which can lead to uneven heating.

### 4. Quality & Durability - What to look for in construction
While you are looking for a cheap option, you do not want it to feel flimsy. A high-quality stainless steel microwave should have a sturdy door handle or a responsive push-button release. The interior should be made of easy-to-clean materials, preferably with a high-quality coating that resists odors. Check reviews for reports of long-term reliability and how well the turntable motor holds up under heavy loads.

### 5. Brand Reputation - Importance of established manufacturers
Stick to brands with a proven track record like Toshiba, Black+Decker, GE, and Frigidaire. These companies offer better warranties and have established customer service channels. Buying from a reputable brand ensures that you are getting a product that meets safety standards like UL certification, and you will have an easier time finding replacement parts like glass turntables if one ever breaks.

### 6. Portability/Size - Considerations for space and storage
Measure your designated microwave spot twice. Do not forget to account for the depth of the unit, including the cord and the clearance needed for the door to open fully. Compact models (0.7 to 0.9 cu ft) are great for dorms and small offices, while 1.1 to 2.0 cu ft units are better suited for permanent kitchen countertops where space is less of an issue.
