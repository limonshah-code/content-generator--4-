## What to Look for Before Buying the Best Propane Tankless Water Heater For High Altitude

### 1. Find Your Need - Compatibility and use case considerations
Before you purchase, determine if you are replacing an existing 6-gallon or 10-gallon tank. Most tankless units are designed to fit the 12.8 x 12.8-inch cutout of common Suburban or Atwood models. If you have a larger 10-gallon tank, you will likely need a retrofit door kit (18 x 18 inches). Also, consider your lifestyle: if you frequently wash gear or pets outside, look for a model with dual outlets or an included external showerhead.

### 2. Budget - Setting realistic price expectations ($400-$800 ranges)
Propane tankless water heaters for RVs generally fall into the $400 to $800 range. Lower-priced models often provide around 55,000 BTU, while premium models with mixing tanks, NIDEC fans, and higher BTU (65,000-68,000) will be at the top of that range. Remember to factor in whether a door is included in the price, as separate door kits can cost an additional $50 to $100.

### 3. Key Features - Most important technical specifications
For high-altitude use, the most critical features are the fan type and combustion technology. Look for brushless DC fans or specialized NIDEC fans that can maintain air-fuel mixtures in thin air. Staged combustion is also vital; it allows the heater to use fewer burner rows in the summer to prevent overheating and all rows in the winter for maximum warmth.

### 4. Quality & Durability - What to look for in construction
An oxygen-free copper heat exchanger is the gold standard for durability. It resists corrosion better than standard materials and offers superior thermal conductivity. Additionally, ensure the unit has a robust anti-freeze function. If you travel in cold climates, the unit should automatically heat itself when temperatures drop below 43 degrees to prevent pipe damage.

### 5. Brand Reputation - Importance of established manufacturers
Brands like Ranein, Fogatti, and Poukaran have established themselves in the RV market by focusing specifically on the challenges of mobile life. Look for brands that offer at least a 24-month warranty and have a clear system for displaying error codes. This makes troubleshooting in remote areas much easier.

### 6. Portability/Size - Considerations for space and storage
The weight and size of the unit are important for maintaining your RV's fuel efficiency. Tankless heaters are significantly lighter than full 6-gallon tanks (which weigh about 50 lbs when full). Most tankless units weigh between 20 and 30 lbs, offering a significant weight saving that can be used for other supplies.

## Is it A Wise Decision to Buy propane tankless water heater for high altitude?

Investing in a propane tankless water heater designed for high altitude is an exceptionally wise decision for any serious traveler. Standard RV water heaters often suffer from flame-out or inefficient heating when oxygen levels drop at higher elevations. By choosing a unit specifically optimized for up to 9,800 feet, you ensure that your comfort isn't dictated by your location.

Beyond the altitude benefits, these units offer an endless supply of hot water. Unlike traditional 6-gallon tanks that run out after one short shower, a tankless unit keeps the hot water flowing as long as you have propane and a water source. This is a massive quality-of-life upgrade for families or couples traveling together. Furthermore, the weight savings and propane efficiency of staged combustion models make it a cost-effective long-term investment. While the initial price is higher than a standard tank, the comfort, convenience, and reliability in mountain environments make it a necessary upgrade for the modern adventurer.

## What Is the Best Way to Choose propane tankless water heater for high altitude?

Choosing the right heater involves a systematic approach to ensure compatibility and performance. Follow these steps:

1. **Check the Altitude Rating:** Ensure the unit is explicitly rated for 9,800 feet (about 3,000 meters). Many budget units are only rated for 2,000-5,000 feet and will fail in the high mountains.
2. **Measure Your Cutout:** Open your exterior water heater door and measure the rough opening. Most are 12.8 x 12.8 inches, but you must verify this before ordering.
3. **Verify Voltage Requirements:** These units require 12V DC power to run the fan and the computer board. Ensure your RV's electrical system is healthy and can handle the draw.
4. **Look for a Mixing Tank:** If you want the most residential-like experience, prioritize models like the Fogatti or Ranein that include a mixing chamber or tank. This prevents the initial blast of cold water.
5. **Decide on Exterior Color:** Choose a unit that comes with a door matching your RV (usually black or white) to save yourself the hassle of painting it later.
6. **Evaluate the Remote:** Decide if you want a wired remote (more reliable connection) or a wireless/magnetic remote (easier to place anywhere).

Avoid the mistake of buying a unit based solely on BTU. While high BTU is good, a unit with 65,000 BTU and a high-altitude fan will outperform a 70,000 BTU unit that isn't optimized for thin air.

## FAQ

**What is the best propane tankless water heater for high altitude for beginners?**
The Nessxa 65K BTU is an excellent choice for beginners. It features a standardized design that fits most common RV cutouts without modification and comes with a detailed, easy-to-follow installation guide. Its staged combustion technology also makes it very forgiving for those who aren't yet experts at managing propane levels.

**How much should I spend on propane tankless water heater for high altitude?**
You should expect to spend between $450 and $650 for a high-quality unit. While there are cheaper options, spending a bit more ensures you get a unit with a high-altitude rated fan and a copper heat exchanger, both of which are critical for longevity and performance in the mountains.

**What features are most important in propane tankless water heater for high altitude?**
The most important features are a high-altitude optimized fan (like a brushless DC or NIDEC fan), an oxygen-free copper heat exchanger for durability, and a thermostatic mixing chamber to ensure the water temperature stays consistent even when water pressure fluctuates.

**How do I maintain my propane tankless water heater for high altitude?**
Maintenance involves descaling the unit at least once a year, especially if you camp in areas with hard water. You should also periodically check the exhaust vent for debris or insect nests and ensure the 12V connections remain clean and tight to prevent power drops to the control board.

**Are expensive propane tankless water heater for high altitude worth it?**
Yes, expensive models often include a mixing tank or chamber which eliminates the 'cold water sandwich' effect. They also tend to have better wind resistance and more advanced safety certifications like CSA, which provide peace of mind and a more home-like showering experience.

**What brands make the best propane tankless water heater for high altitude?**
Ranein, Fogatti, and APUS are currently the top brands in this niche. They have specifically updated their product lines (Gen II and Gen 3 models) to include fans and combustion controllers that are specifically tuned for the thinner air found at high elevations.

**How long do propane tankless water heater for high altitude typically last?**
With proper maintenance and winterization, a high-quality propane tankless water heater can last 10 to 15 years. This is significantly longer than many traditional tank heaters, which are prone to rusting out or leaking after 6 to 8 years of use.

**Can I find good propane tankless water heater for high altitude on a budget?**
Yes, the APUS 68K BTU unit offers incredible power and high-altitude performance at a very competitive price. While it might lack some of the ultra-premium features like a NIDEC fan, it still uses a copper heat exchanger and provides excellent value for the money.
