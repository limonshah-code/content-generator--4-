## What to Look for Before Buying the Best Mattress Australian Made

### Find Your Need
Before you start browsing, you must identify your primary sleep style. Side sleepers generally need more sinkage for their hips and shoulders to maintain a straight spine, which means a softer or medium-plush mattress is usually best. Back and stomach sleepers, however, require a firmer surface to prevent their midsections from dipping too low, which can lead to chronic lower back pain. If you share a bed, prioritize motion isolation features like pocketed coils or memory foam.

### Budget
The price of a high-quality mattress can vary significantly based on the materials used. You can find excellent budget-friendly memory foam mattresses in the $300-$600 range for a Queen size. Mid-range hybrids that combine foam and springs typically fall between $600-$1,200. Premium options that utilize organic latex, wool, and high-density coils often exceed $1,500. Setting a realistic budget helps you narrow down whether you are looking for pure value or a long-term organic investment.

### Key Features
Temperature regulation is a vital feature for many. Look for gel infusions, copper, or natural wool if you tend to sleep hot. Support systems also matter; while all-foam mattresses offer great contouring, hybrids often provide better edge support and airflow. Consider the height of the mattress as well, as some people prefer a low-profile 8-inch bed while others want the luxury of a 14-inch stack.

### Quality & Durability
Check for high-density foams and quality coil counts. A heavier mattress often indicates higher-quality, more durable materials that will resist sagging over time. Certifications like CertiPUR-US for foams and GOTS for organic materials are your best indicators that the mattress is built to last and safe to sleep on. Always look for at least a 10-year warranty as a sign of the manufacturer's confidence.

### Brand Reputation
Buying from a brand with established customer service and clear shipping policies is crucial. Check for trial periods or comfort guarantees, as you won't truly know if a mattress is right for you until you sleep on it for several weeks. Brands that manufacture locally often have better quality control and more transparent material sourcing.

### Portability/Size
Most modern mattresses arrive compressed in a box, making them easy to move through tight hallways and staircases. However, remember that once they expand, they are much heavier and harder to relocate. Ensure your bed frame is compatible with the mattress type—some hybrids require specific slat spacing, while foam beds work best on solid platforms.
