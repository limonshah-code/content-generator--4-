## What to Look for Before Buying the Best Dual Band Router Under 1500

### 1. Find Your Need - Compatibility and use case considerations
Before purchasing, evaluate how many devices you plan to connect. If you have a house full of smart gadgets, look for WiFi 6 or WiFi 7 routers that handle high-density traffic. If you only browse on a laptop and phone, a budget-friendly AC1200 model might be perfectly sufficient.

### 2. Budget - Setting realistic price expectations ($50-$250 ranges)
While our search is for the best value under 1500, prices in the networking world vary by technology. WiFi 5 routers are very affordable ($40-$70), while the latest WiFi 7 models range from $150 to $250. Investing a bit more now can save you from needing an upgrade in two years.

### 3. Key Features - Most important technical specifications
Look for MU-MIMO and Beamforming technology; these ensure that your router talks to multiple devices at once and focuses the signal directly toward them. Also, check for Gigabit ports if you have a high-speed internet plan, as 100 Mbps ports will throttle your speed.

### 4. Quality & Durability - What to look for in construction
A good router should have adequate ventilation to prevent overheating during heavy use. Look for brands like TP-Link, Netgear, and Linksys, which are known for long-lasting internal components and frequent firmware updates that extend the life of the hardware.

### 5. Brand Reputation - Importance of established manufacturers
Stick to established brands that offer reliable customer support and regular security patches. Companies that sign pledges like the CISA Secure-by-Design show a commitment to your long-term online safety, which is vital in a world of increasing cyber threats.

### 6. Portability/Size - Considerations for space and storage
Modern routers come in various shapes, from flat horizontal units with many antennas to sleek vertical designs. Consider your available shelf space. Vertical designs often have a smaller footprint and can fit better in tight areas like bookshelves.
