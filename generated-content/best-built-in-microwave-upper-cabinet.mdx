## What to Look for Before Buying the Best Built In Microwave Upper Cabinet

### 1. Find Your Need - Compatibility and use case considerations
Before you start shopping, you must measure your existing cabinet opening or consult your kitchen plans. Built-in microwaves come in standard widths like 24, 27, and 30 inches. You also need to decide between a traditional swing door, a drop-down door, or a modern drawer style. Drawer styles are excellent for island installations or lower cabinets, while drop-down doors are perfect for upper wall cabinets where they can mimic the look of a secondary oven.

### 2. Budget - Setting realistic price expectations ($X-$Y ranges)
Setting a budget is essential, as prices vary significantly based on features. Basic built-in models with trim kits typically range from $300 to $600. If you are looking for advanced drawer-style microwaves or convection combos, expect to pay between $800 and $1,500. Luxury brands with specialized sensor technology can go even higher. Remember to factor in potential costs for professional installation and cabinet modifications.

### 3. Key Features - Most important technical specifications
Look for a unit with at least 1000 watts of power for efficient cooking. Sensor cooking is a must-have feature for many, as it adjusts time and power automatically by measuring steam. If you plan to use the unit as a secondary oven, seek out convection or air fry modes. Also, consider the internal capacity; a 1.2 cubic foot unit is standard for drawers, while 2.2 cubic feet is better for large family portions.

### 4. Quality & Durability - What to look for in construction
Stainless steel is the gold standard for durability and ease of cleaning. Check if the interior is also stainless steel, which lasts longer than painted metal. Examine the door hinges and the smoothness of the drawer glide; these moving parts are the most common points of failure. Units with soft-close mechanisms tend to experience less mechanical stress over time, extending the life of the appliance.

### 5. Brand Reputation - Importance of established manufacturers
Choosing a brand like Frigidaire, Zephyr, or COSMO often ensures better access to replacement parts and customer support. Established manufacturers provide more detailed installation guides and have verified safety certifications. Read user reviews specifically focusing on long-term reliability and how the brand handles warranty claims, as these appliances are meant to last 10 years or more.

### 6. Portability/Size - Considerations for space and storage
Since these are built-in units, portability is not a factor, but precision in size is. Ensure you have the required depth for the unit to sit flush against your cabinetry. Some units require extra space for venting, either through the front or a dedicated duct. If you are installing in an upper cabinet, make sure the cabinet is rated to hold the weight of the microwave, which can be 50 to 80 pounds.
