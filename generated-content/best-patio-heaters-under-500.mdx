## What to Look for Before Buying the Best Patio Heaters Under 500

### Find Your Need - Compatibility and use case considerations
Before you purchase, determine if you need to heat a large open area or a small personal space. Propane heaters like the East Oak are best for wide-open backyards where no electricity is available. Electric options like the Nessxa or HETRAROOT are better for covered porches, garages, or indoor/outdoor transitions where you have access to a wall outlet.

### Budget - Setting realistic price expectations ($50-$500 ranges)
In the $50 to $150 range, you will find excellent personal ceramic heaters and some basic electric tower units. As you move toward the $250 to $500 range, you enter the territory of high-output propane pyramid heaters and professional wall-mounted infrared units. Setting a budget helps you decide between the convenience of electric or the raw power of propane.

### Key Features - Most important technical specifications
Look for the BTU rating on propane units (aim for 40,000+) or the Wattage on electric units (1500W is the standard for high power). If you live in a windy area, prioritized weighted bases or units that can be bolted down. For electric models, oscillation and remote controls are features that significantly improve the daily user experience.

### Quality & Durability - What to look for in construction
For propane heaters, stainless steel (especially 304 grade) is the gold standard for resisting rust. For electric heaters, look for an IP rating (like IPX5 or IP55) if you plan to use them in damp or open-air environments. Powder-coated aluminum is another excellent material that balances weight and weather resistance.

### Brand Reputation - Importance of established manufacturers
Choosing brands like East Oak, Diamondforge, or Amazon Basics ensures you have access to customer support and standardized safety certifications like CSA or ETL. Established brands are more likely to provide accurate BTU and safety claims, which is critical when dealing with high-heat appliances.

### Portability/Size - Considerations for space and storage
If you need to store your heater during the summer, consider the footprint. Pyramid heaters are tall and don't collapse, while mini ceramic heaters can fit in a drawer. Units with built-in wheels are essential if you plan to move a large propane heater across your deck frequently.
