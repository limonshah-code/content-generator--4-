## What to Look for Before Buying the Best Amora Lighting Wall Fixtures

### 1. Find Your Need - Compatibility and use case considerations
Before purchasing, consider where the fixture will live. Stained glass fixtures like Amora are heavy and require a secure wall mount. Ensure your existing electrical box can handle the weight. Also, decide if you need focused task lighting or ambient mood lighting, as the color of the glass will significantly impact the brightness and tone of the room.

### 2. Budget - Setting realistic price expectations ($X-$Y ranges)
Amora and similar tiffany-style fixtures typically range from $60 to $150 per sconce. Higher prices often reflect a higher count of hand-cut glass pieces or additional jewels. Modern LED alternatives are usually more affordable, often falling in the $40 to $80 range, but lack the handcrafted artisan feel of stained glass.

### 3. Key Features - Most important technical specifications
Look for dual-light designs if you want to illuminate both the floor and ceiling. Check for LED compatibility to ensure you can use energy-efficient bulbs. The extension from the wall is also crucial; if the lamp sticks out too far in a narrow hallway, it could be a hazard.

### 4. Quality & Durability - What to look for in construction
High-quality stained glass should have smooth soldering lines and no loose pieces. The metal base should be heavy and rust-proof. Authentic Amora fixtures use real glass, which you can test by tapping it gently—it should sound different than plastic alternatives.

### 5. Brand Reputation - Importance of established manufacturers
Amora Lighting and Kichler are established names in the industry. Buying from a reputable brand ensures that the product meets safety standards like UL or ETL listing. It also means you are more likely to get consistent color across multiple units if you are buying a set.

### 6. Portability/Size - Considerations for space and storage
