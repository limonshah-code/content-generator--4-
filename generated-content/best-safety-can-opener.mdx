## What to Look for Before Buying the Best Safety Can Opener

### Find Your Need - Compatibility and use case considerations
Before you purchase, consider the types of cans you open most often. If you handle a lot of pet food or soup with ring-pull tabs, look for a model with a built-in tab lifter. If you have limited counter space, a manual handheld model or a small battery-operated one might be better than a tall electric unit. Consider if you need a tool that can also handle bottles and jars.

### Budget - Setting realistic price expectations ($X-$Y ranges)
Manual safety openers typically range from $15 to $25. These are durable and reliable for most homes. Battery-operated automatic models usually cost between $20 and $35, offering more convenience. High-end rechargeable electric models or premium countertop units can range from $35 to $60. Setting a budget helps you decide if you want to pay more for features like USB charging or multi-tool functionality.

### Key Features - Most important technical specifications
The most critical feature is the side-cut technology, which ensures no sharp edges. Other features to look for include a magnetic lid lifter to keep your hands clean and oversized handles or knobs for easier turning. If you choose an electric model, look for an auto-stop feature that prevents the machine from going around the can multiple times and potentially creating metal shards.

### Quality & Durability - What to look for in construction
Look for stainless steel components, especially for the cutting wheels. Chrome construction is also excellent for manual tools. High-quality plastics should feel dense and robust rather than thin and brittle. Check for rust-resistance if you live in a humid environment or plan to use the opener for camping.

### Brand Reputation - Importance of established manufacturers
