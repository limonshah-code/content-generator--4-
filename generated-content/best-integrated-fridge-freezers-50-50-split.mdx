Fridge freezers vary wildly in price. Compact models typically range from $250 to $600. Mid-sized apartment units usually sit between $600 and $1,200. ### 3. Key Features - Most important technical specifications
Look for automatic defrosting; it saves hours of maintenance. Digital temperature controls are superior to manual dials for precision. LED lighting is a must-have for visibility. Additionally, check for reversible doors; this small feature can make or break your kitchen layout if you have a tight corner.

### 4. Quality & Durability - What to look for in construction
Stainless steel is the gold standard for durability and ease of cleaning. Tempered glass shelves are much stronger and easier to clean than wire racks. Check the compressor type; inverter compressors are generally quieter and more durable than traditional models. High-quality magnetic gaskets are also vital for keeping the cold air inside.

### 5. Brand Reputation - Importance of established manufacturers
Stick with brands that offer solid warranties and accessible customer service. Brands like Kenmore and Frigidaire have decades of history, while newer players like Upstreman and BODEGA have carved out niches in the compact and portable markets. Always check if the manufacturer offers on-site service or easy replacement parts.

### 6. Portability/Size - Considerations for space and storage
Measure your doorway, hallway, and the final destination space twice. Many people forget to account for the door swing clearance. If you plan on moving the unit, look for models with built-in casters or wheels. For RV or travel use, weight and 12V compatibility are the most critical factors to consider.
