## What to Look for Before Buying the Best Navy Blue Air Fryer

### Find Your Need - Compatibility and use case considerations
Before purchasing, consider how many people you typically cook for. A 4-quart model is perfect for 1-2 people, while a 5-6 quart model is better for a family of four. If you plan to cook entire meals at once, look for a dual-zone or TriZone model. Additionally, ensure the blue shade matches your existing decor, as navy can vary from deep, almost-black tones to brighter sapphire hues.

### Budget - Setting realistic price expectations ($X-$Y ranges)
Air fryers typically fall into three price brackets. Budget models ($50-$80) offer basic controls and reliable frying. Mid-range models ($80-$150) often include digital displays, more presets, and better materials like ceramic coatings. Premium models ($150-$250+) offer high-speed motors, larger capacities, and multi-functional capabilities like rotisserie or proofing.

### Key Features - Most important technical specifications
Look for a wide temperature range, ideally from 90F (for dehydrating) to 400F or 450F (for maximum crisping). Pre-set functions can save time, while features like 'Sync Finish' or 'Shake Alarms' improve the cooking experience. Transparency is another great feature; glass pots allow you to see your food's progress without losing heat.

### Quality & Durability - What to look for in construction
