## What to Look for Before Buying the Best Angle For Sharpening Kitchen Knives

### 1. Find Your Need - Compatibility and use case considerations
Before you purchase a guide or system, identify the types of knives you own. European and American knives typically use a 20-degree angle for durability, while Japanese or Asian-style blades often require a 15-degree angle for precision slicing. Ensure the tool you choose supports the specific degrees your blades need. Also, consider the blade size; some guides only clip onto large chef knives, while others are universal.

### 2. Budget - Setting realistic price expectations
You can find basic clip-on guides for under $15, which are great for learning the basics. Mid-range kits that include whetstones and bamboo bases usually fall between $30 and $60. If you are looking for professional, highly adjustable systems with multiple abrasives, expect to spend between $100 and $250. Investing more usually gets you better repeatability and higher-quality materials like stainless steel or diamond.

### 3. Key Features - Most important technical specifications
Look for adjustable angles if you have a diverse knife collection. Consistency is the most important factor in sharpening, so a tool that locks the angle in place is superior to one that relies on hand-eye coordination.
