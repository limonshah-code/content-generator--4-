## What to Look for Before Buying the Best 2 Tap Kegerator

### Find Your Need - Compatibility and use case considerations
Before you buy, decide if you will be using store-bought kegs or homebrew kegs. Store-bought kegs typically use a D-system coupler, while homebrewers usually need ball-lock or pin-lock couplers. If you want variety, ensure the unit can fit two 1/6 barrels or two slim quarters. If you plan to build it into your kitchen, you must look for a front-venting model.

### Budget - Setting realistic price expectations ($X-$Y ranges)
A basic 2-tap kegerator usually starts around $450 to $600. These are typically freestanding units with manual controls. Professional-grade, built-in, or outdoor-rated units can range from $800 to $1,500. DIY kits are the most affordable way to start if you already have a refrigerator, usually costing between $150 and $300.

### Key Features - Most important technical specifications
Look for fan-forced cooling, as this prevents temperature layers and keeps the tower cold. Digital temperature displays are much easier to manage than manual knobs. A dual-gauge regulator is also a huge plus, as it tells you both the pressure going to the beer and how much gas is left in the tank.

### Quality & Durability - What to look for in construction
A stainless steel floor plate is essential to protect the interior from heavy kegs. Check the material of the faucets; stainless steel is better than chrome-plated brass because it won't leach metallic flavors into your beer and is more resistant to acidic beverages like wine or cider.

### Brand Reputation - Importance of established manufacturers
Brands like Kegco and EdgeStar have been in the industry for years and offer excellent customer support and replacement parts. Buying from an established brand ensures that if a seal breaks or you need a new coupler in five years, you will actually be able to find the parts.

### Portability/Size - Considerations for space and storage
Measure your space twice. Most units are about 24 inches wide, but some slim models are closer to 20 inches. If you plan to move the unit between your garage and patio, ensure it has heavy-duty locking casters. Remember that CO2 tanks can sometimes be mounted externally to save room inside the cabinet.

## Is it A Wise Decision to Buy 2 tap kegerator?

Investing in a 2-tap kegerator is a fantastic decision for anyone who regularly entertains or enjoys a variety of craft beers. The primary advantage is the ability to have two different styles of beer on tap simultaneously, such as a light lager for hot days and a heavy stout for the evenings. This variety significantly enhances the experience compared to a single-tap system.

Furthermore, buying beer in kegs is often more cost-effective and environmentally friendly than buying cases of cans or bottles. You eliminate the waste of packaging and can often save 30% to 50% on the per-pint cost of high-end craft beer. While the initial investment is higher, the long-term enjoyment and savings make it a wise purchase for the dedicated enthusiast. Compared to going to a bar, having a draft system at home pays for itself within a year or two of regular use.

## What Is the Best Way to Choose 2 tap kegerator?

To choose the best unit, start by measuring your available space and determining if you need a freestanding or built-in model. This is the biggest factor that will dictate your price range. Once you have the space requirements, look at the cooling technology. We highly recommend fan-forced cooling over static cooling to ensure your beer stays at a consistent temperature from top to bottom.

Next, evaluate the tapping hardware. Many budget units come with low-quality plastic parts that can lead to leaks. Look for kits that include metal couplers and stainless steel faucets. Finally, consider the warranty and brand history. A longer warranty on the compressor is a good sign of a quality manufacturer. Don't forget to check if the CO2 tank is included, as this can be a hidden $60-$80 cost if you have to buy it separately.
