## What to Look for Before Buying the Best 2 Tap Kegerator

### Find Your Need - Compatibility and use case considerations
Before you purchase, you must determine what types of kegs you intend to serve. Most residential units are designed for D-system Sankey kegs, which cover the vast majority of commercial North American beers. However, if you are a homebrewer, you will likely need ball-lock or pin-lock connectors. Some models are more versatile than others, fitting three small homebrew kegs, while others are limited to two sixth barrels. Consider whether you need a freestanding unit or a built-in model that can be installed under a counter.

### Budget - Setting realistic price expectations ($X-$Y ranges)
The cost of a quality dual-tap system typically ranges from $500 for a basic freestanding unit to $1,500 or more for professional, commercial-grade models. If you are on a budget, you might consider a conversion kit for $150 to $300, provided you already have a functioning refrigerator. Keep in mind that hidden costs, such as CO2 tank refills and cleaning supplies, should be factored into your long-term budget.

### Key Features - Most important technical specifications
Look for fan-forced cooling and digital temperature controls, as these ensure the beer stays at the exact temperature you prefer. An air-cooled draft tower is another high-value feature, as it prevents the beer in the lines from warming up, which is the leading cause of excess foam during the first pour. Finally, check the temperature range; if you plan to serve stouts or use the unit for fermentation, you will need a wider range than just the standard 32-40 degrees.

### Quality & Durability - What to look for in construction
Stainless steel is the gold standard for kegerator construction. Ideally, your dispensing system should have 100 percent stainless steel contact components to prevent off-flavors and corrosion. A stainless steel floor plate is also essential, as heavy kegs can easily crack plastic linings over time. Check the quality of the casters as well; they should be heavy-duty and locking to ensure the unit stays put once positioned.

### Brand Reputation - Importance of established manufacturers
Brands like Kegco and EdgeStar have been in the industry for years and offer extensive warranties and replacement parts. Buying from an established brand ensures that you can find new gaskets, couplers, or faucets years down the road. Newer brands like VEVOR offer excellent value, but you should verify their customer support and part availability for your specific region.

### Portability/Size - Considerations for space and storage
Measure your intended space carefully. Remember that kegerators need a few inches of clearance on the sides and back for proper airflow and heat dissipation unless they are specifically designed for built-in use. If you plan to use the unit for parties in different areas of your home, ensure it comes with high-quality locking casters.

## Is it A Wise Decision to Buy 2 tap kegerator?

Investing in a dual-tap kegerator is a wise decision for anyone who frequently enjoys draft beer or hosts social gatherings. From a financial perspective, buying beer by the keg is significantly cheaper per pint than purchasing cans or bottles, especially for craft beer enthusiasts. Over a year or two, the savings on the beer itself can often pay for the unit.

Furthermore, a 2-tap system offers a level of variety that a single-tap cannot provide. You can offer a light lager and a heavy IPA simultaneously, catering to different palates among your guests. Beyond the social and financial benefits, kegerators are environmentally friendly because they drastically reduce the amount of waste from cans and glass bottles. When properly maintained, these units last for many years and can even add value to your home bar setup. While the initial investment is higher than a standard fridge, the quality of the beverage and the convenience of having draft beer on demand make it a worthwhile addition to any home.

## What Is the Best Way to Choose 2 tap kegerator?

The best way to choose a 2 tap kegerator is to follow a systematic approach that matches your specific drinking habits with the technical capabilities of the machine. Start by measuring the space where you intend to put the unit and determining if you need a front-venting model for under-counter installation or a side/rear-venting model for freestanding use.

Next, prioritize the cooling technology. We highly recommend units with fan-forced cooling and digital thermostats because they eliminate the guesswork and ensure your beer is served at its optimal temperature. If you can afford it, always opt for a model with an air-cooled tower to reduce foam waste. 

Common mistakes to avoid include buying a unit that doesn't fit the specific kegs you want (like Coors or Miller rubberized kegs) and ignoring the material of the faucets. If you are a beginner, look for a complete kit that includes the CO2 tank and all necessary hoses to avoid the frustration of missing parts. Finally, if you are an experienced homebrewer, look for models with extra internal volume to accommodate multiple slim kegs.
