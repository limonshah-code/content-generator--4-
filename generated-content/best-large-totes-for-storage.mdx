## What to Look for Before Buying the Best Large Totes For Storage

### Find Your Need
Before purchasing, determine if you need the totes for moving, long-term attic storage, or daily closet access. Moving requires flexible bags with strong handles, while garage storage usually calls for rigid plastic bins that can stack without collapsing.

### Budget
Storage solutions can range from $30 for a basic set of fabric bags to over $150 for a large pallet of industrial bins. Setting a budget helps you decide between bulk packs that offer better value per unit or premium single units with specialized latching features.

### Key Features
Look for reinforced handles, heavy-duty zippers, and secure lid latches. If you need to see your items, prioritize clear plastic or bags with transparent windows. For heavy items, ensure the product has a weight rating of at least 60 pounds.

### Quality & Durability
Plastic bins should be made of high-density polyethylene or polypropylene to resist cracking. Fabric bags should have a high GMS (grams per square meter) rating and double-stitched handles to ensure they don't tear when lifted.

### Brand Reputation
Brands like Sterilite, Iris USA, and SpaceAid have established a history of reliability. Buying from a known manufacturer often means better quality control and easier access to customer support if a lid arrives damaged or a zipper fails.

### Portability/Size
Consider the dimensions of your storage space. Measure your closet shelves or the height of your under-bed area before buying. Also, check for features like backpack straps or ergonomic grips if you plan on moving the totes frequently.

## What Is the Best Way to Choose large totes for storage?

To choose the best storage tote, start by categorizing the items you need to store. Heavier items like tools or books should go into smaller, rigid plastic bins to prevent the weight from becoming unmanageable. Soft items like comforters and seasonal clothing are best suited for large, breathable fabric bags that can be squeezed into tight spaces.

Next, consider the environment where the totes will live. If you are storing items in a damp basement or a dusty garage, look for bins with gasket seals or heavy-duty snap-fit lids. For climate-controlled closets, clear plastic bins or fabric bags with windows are preferable so you can quickly find your wardrobe items. Always check the stackability of the product; if the lids are not recessed, the stack may become unstable and dangerous. Finally, prioritize products made in the USA or those with BPA-free certifications to ensure you are bringing safe materials into your home environment.
