The first step is determining if your kitchen supports an external vent or if you strictly need a ductless model. A 30 inch ductless range hood is perfect for those who cannot cut holes in their walls or ceilings. Consider your cooking habits; if you fry food frequently, you will need a unit with higher CFM and multi-layer filtration. If you mostly boil water or steam vegetables, a lower-powered, quieter model will suffice.

### 2. Budget - Setting realistic price expectations ($X-$Y ranges)
Range hoods in this category typically fall into three price brackets. Entry-level models range from $80 to $130 and offer basic 2-speed fans and simple mesh filters. Mid-range options from $130 to $250 often include better lighting, stainless steel finishes, and more powerful motors. Premium models, costing $250 to $500+, provide features like gesture controls, extremely high CFM (600-900), and advanced baffle filters.

### 3. Key Features - Most important technical specifications
Look for the CFM (Cubic Feet per Minute) rating, which tells you how much air the fan moves. For ductless models, anything between 200 and 400 CFM is standard, while 600+ is considered high performance. Noise levels, measured in Sones or Decibels, are also crucial. A rating of 1 to 3 Sones is quiet, while anything over 6 Sones can be quite loud. Additionally, check if the lights are LED or if you need to buy bulbs separately.

### 4. Quality & Durability - What to look for in construction
High-quality range hoods are typically made from 430-grade stainless steel, which is resistant to corrosion and heat. Check for hemmed edges or mitered sides, as these indicate better manufacturing and safer handling during cleaning. The motor is the heart of the unit; look for brands that mention copper motors or offer extended warranties on the motor specifically.

### 5. Brand Reputation - Importance of established manufacturers
Choosing a brand like Broan-NuTone, CIARRA, or COSMO ensures that you will have access to replacement filters and customer support in the future. Established brands often have better quality control and more accurate performance ratings. Read user reviews to see how the company handles warranty claims and if replacement parts are easy to find on sites like Amazon.

### 6. Portability/Size - Considerations for space and storage
The 30-inch width is standard for most residential stoves, but the height and depth can vary. Ultra-slim models (under 4 inches tall) are ideal if you have low-hanging cabinets. Ensure the depth (usually around 17-20 inches) covers most of your burner area to capture rising smoke effectively. Most under-cabinet hoods are permanent installations, so check the mounting hole patterns to ensure they align with your cabinetry.
