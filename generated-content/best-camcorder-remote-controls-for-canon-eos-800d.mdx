## What to Look for Before Buying the Best Camcorder Remote Controls For Canon Eos 800d

### 1. Find Your Need - Compatibility and use case considerations
The first step is ensuring the remote is compatible with the Canon EOS 800D (Rebel T7i). This camera uses Bluetooth technology for wireless control, but it can also use 2.4GHz radio remotes if they include a receiver that plugs into the camera jack. If you are doing solo vlogging, a Bluetooth remote is usually better because it is small and requires no extra hardware on the camera. For landscape or sports photography where you might be far from the camera, a radio frequency (RF) remote with a receiver offers much greater range and reliability through obstacles.

### 2. Budget - Setting realistic price expectations ($15-$60)
Remotes for the 800D are generally affordable. Basic third-party Bluetooth remotes can be found for as little as $15 to $20. Official Canon remotes usually sit in the $40 to $50 range. If you want high-end features like OLED screens and rechargeable batteries, expect to pay between $30 and $60. Setting a budget helps you decide if the convenience of a rechargeable battery is worth the extra $15 over a model that uses disposable button cells.

### 3. Key Features - Most important technical specifications
Look for remotes that offer more than just a shutter button. A dedicated AF (autofocus) button is vital for ensuring your subject is sharp before the shot. If you record video, make sure the remote has a specific start/stop toggle. Zoom control is also a nice feature, though it usually requires specific power-zoom lenses or adapters. Finally, check for a 2-second delay mode, which is helpful for hiding the remote in your hand before the shutter fires in a self-portrait.

### 4. Quality & Durability - What to look for in construction
Since remotes are often tossed into bags or used outdoors, build quality matters. Look for high-density plastics and buttons that offer tactile feedback. If you shoot in cold or wet conditions, weather-sealed options are rare but worth looking for. Even a basic remote should feel solid in the hand; avoid anything that feels hollow or has buttons that wiggle excessively, as these are signs of poor internal construction.

### 5. Brand Reputation - Importance of established manufacturers
While the official Canon brand offers the highest peace of mind, companies like AODELAN and PROfezzion have built strong reputations in the photography community. These brands often provide features that the official remote lacks, such as rechargeable batteries or OLED displays. Reading user reviews is essential to ensure the manufacturer stands behind their product and offers firmware updates if camera software changes.
