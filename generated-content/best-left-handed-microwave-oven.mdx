## What to Look for Before Buying the Best Left Handed Microwave Oven

### 1. Find Your Need - Compatibility and use case considerations
When looking for a microwave that works well for diverse users, consider how the door opens and where the controls are located. While most microwaves are hinged on the left, a push-button door release is often much easier to operate than a handle. Think about whether you will be using it for heavy cooking or just quick reheating, as this will dictate the size and power you need.

### 2. Budget - Setting realistic price expectations
Microwave prices vary significantly based on features and power. Basic compact models (700W-900W) usually range from $70 to $120. Mid-range models with sensor technology and more space typically cost between $130 and $200. High-end multi-functional units that include air fry or convection capabilities can range from $200 to $400 or more.

### 3. Key Features - Most important technical specifications
Look for features that enhance usability, such as a mute button for silent operation and a 30-second quick start. Sensor cooking is another vital feature as it prevents overcooking by measuring steam or humidity. For safety, a child lock is essential, especially in households with curious little ones.

### 4. Quality & Durability - What to look for in construction
