## What to Look for Before Buying the Best Callan2 All Door Locks

### 1. Find Your Need - Compatibility and use case considerations
Before making a purchase, identify the specific door you are securing. Exterior doors require keyed entry or deadbolts for maximum safety, while interior doors might only need privacy locks. Ensure the lock you choose is compatible with your door thickness, which usually ranges from 1-3/8 to 1-3/4 inches. Also, check the backset measurement of your door to ensure the latch will fit properly.

### 2. Budget - Setting realistic price expectations ($15-$45 ranges)
Standard residential locks typically fall between $15 and $30. If you are looking for premium finishes like oil rubbed bronze or advanced features like SmartKey technology, you may spend closer to $45. Setting a budget early helps you decide between a basic knob or a more ergonomic lever without overspending on features you might not need.

### 3. Key Features - Most important technical specifications
Look for features that simplify your life. Reversible or non-handed handles are essential if you aren’t sure of your door swing. For high-security areas, look for deadbolts with 1-inch throws and hardened steel bolts. If hygiene is a priority, consider options with antimicrobial coatings like Microban to keep your home cleaner.

### 4. Quality & Durability - What to look for in construction
Metal construction is a must for any lock intended for an exterior door. Avoid plastic components that can easily break or be bypassed. A high-quality finish, such as satin nickel or oil rubbed bronze, should be resistant to tarnishing and weather conditions to ensure your hardware looks good for years to come.

### 5. Brand Reputation - Importance of established manufacturers
Choosing brands like Delaney, Callan, or Kwikset ensures you are getting a product backed by years of engineering and customer support. Established brands often offer better warranties and have replacement parts readily available if something goes wrong during or after installation.

### 6. Portability/Size - Considerations for space and storage
While locks aren't portable in the traditional sense, their size matters for installation. Standardized sizes make it easy to swap out old locks without drilling new holes. Ensure the strike plates and latch assemblies match your existing door cutouts to save time and effort during the setup process.
