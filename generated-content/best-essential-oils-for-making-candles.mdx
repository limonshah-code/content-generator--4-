## What to Look for Before Buying the Best Essential Oils For Making Candles

### Find Your Need
Before purchasing, consider what type of wax you are using and what mood you want to create. Soy wax often requires a higher concentration of oil compared to paraffin to achieve the same scent throw. If you are making gifts, a variety set like the MAYJAM 20-piece collection is ideal. For specific high-end product lines, choosing a themed set like the P&J Gentlemen or Nature sets might be more appropriate.

### Budget
Fragrance oils range from $15 for a basic set to over $50 for large bulk bottles. Sampler sets of 5ml or 10ml bottles usually cost between $1.50 and $3.00 per bottle. If you are a high-volume maker, investing in 100ml bottles like the PHATOIL series can drop your cost per candle significantly. Set a realistic expectation based on whether this is a one-time hobby or a regular crafting activity.

### Key Features
Look for the flash point of the oil, which should ideally be above 200 degrees for candle making to ensure the scent does not evaporate during the pouring process. Euro droppers are a essential feature for precision, and amber glass bottles are mandatory for protecting the oil from UV degradation. Phthalate-free certifications are also a major plus for safety and air quality.

### Quality & Durability
Pure essential oils are natural but can sometimes be less stable in high heat than synthetic fragrance oils specifically designed for candles. If you want a scent that lasts for a long time on the shelf, look for products with stabilizers or high-quality extraction methods like steam distillation. UV-coated bottles, as seen with PHATOIL, also contribute to the long-term durability of the fragrance.

### Brand Reputation
Established brands like P&J Trading and Lagunamoon have decades of experience and follow IFRA standards. These brands are more likely to provide consistent results and accurate scent profiles. Buying from reputable manufacturers ensures that you are getting a product that is safe for your home and contains no hidden toxic solvents.

### Portability/Size
If you have a limited workspace, smaller 5ml and 10ml sets are easier to store and organize. However, if you have a dedicated studio, larger 4oz or 100ml bottles are more efficient. Consider how much storage space you have and how often you will be using each specific scent before committing to larger volumes.

## Is it A Wise Decision to Buy essential oils for making candles?

Investing in high-quality essential oils or fragrance oils for candle making is a wise decision for anyone looking to personalize their environment. While store-bought candles can be expensive and often contain paraffin and synthetic scents of unknown origin, making your own gives you complete control over the ingredients. High-quality oils ensure that your candles actually smell like the intended fragrance rather than a chemical imitation.

Furthermore, the long-term benefits include a healthier home environment. By selecting phthalate-free and pure plant-based oils, you reduce the toxins released into your air. Compared to the cost of high-end luxury candles, which can retail for $40 or more, a single set of oils combined with bulk wax can yield dozens of professional-quality candles at a fraction of the price. ## What Is the Best Way to Choose essential oils for making candles?

### Selection Guide
1. Identify your wax type (Soy, Paraffin, Beeswax).
2. Determine the scent load capacity of your wax (usually 6% to 10%).
3. Choose between pure essential oils (natural/therapeutic) or fragrance oils (engineered for performance).
4. Verify the flash point of each oil to ensure compatibility with your melting process.
5. Select a theme or variety pack to test multiple scents at once.

### Professional Tips
- Always test a small batch before making a large run. Scent throw can change once the oil interacts with hot wax.
- Keep a detailed log of how many drops or ounces you use per pound of wax.
- Store your oils in a cool, dark place to prevent the scent from fading.
- Add your oil to the wax at the recommended temperature (usually around 185°F for soy) to ensure proper bonding.

### Common Mistakes
- Adding oil to wax that is too hot, causing the fragrance to burn off.
- Using too much oil, which can cause the candle to smoke or the wax to become soft.
- Buying oils that are not intended for candle making, which may not throw scent when burned.
