## What to Look for Before Buying the Best Electric Dryers Under 800

### Find Your Need - Compatibility and use case considerations
Before you purchase, evaluate your living situation. Are you in a permanent home with a 240V outlet, or are you in an apartment or RV that only has standard 110V/120V outlets? Full-sized dryers like the Kenmore require special wiring, while portable units can be plugged in anywhere. Consider how much laundry you do weekly; a single person may be happy with a 1.5 cu.ft. model, but a family will definitely need at least 3.5 cu.ft. or a full-sized 7.0 cu.ft. machine.

### Budget - Setting realistic price expectations ($200-$700 ranges)
In the under 800 category, you can find everything from high-end portable units to entry-level full-sized dryers. Portable models typically range from $200 to $450. Between $500 and $750, you can find large-capacity, full-sized models from reputable brands like Kenmore. Setting a realistic budget helps you decide if you are paying for capacity, portability, or smart features like moisture sensors.

### Key Features - Most important technical specifications
Look for dryers that offer multiple cycles such as 'Delicate', 'Bulky', and 'Timed Dry'. Advanced features like moisture sensors are incredibly valuable because they stop the dryer once clothes are dry, saving energy and protecting fabrics. If you are looking at portable models, check the wattage; 1500W will dry clothes much faster than 800W, though 800W is more energy-efficient for small, frequent loads.

### Quality & Durability - What to look for in construction
A stainless steel drum is the gold standard for durability. It resists scratches, won not rust over time, and is gentler on your clothes. Also, check the filtration system. A good dryer should have at least a double or triple-layer filter to prevent lint from clogging the machine and potentially causing a fire hazard. Sturdy hinges and a magnetic door catch are also signs of a well-built appliance.

### Brand Reputation - Importance of established manufacturers
While newer brands often offer great value, established names like Kenmore or Costway have a track record of customer support and parts availability. Read recent user reviews to see how the brand handles warranty claims. A brand that includes all mounting hardware and clear instructions usually indicates they care about the end-user experience.

### Portability/Size - Considerations for space and storage
Measure your available space twice. If you plan to wall-mount your dryer, ensure you have a sturdy wall and enough clearance for the exhaust hose. For those in RVs, the weight of the unit (typically 30-60 lbs) is a crucial factor for transport and stability. Consider whether you need a front-load or a top-load design based on where the unit will be placed, such as under a counter or stacked on a washer.

## Is it A Wise Decision to Buy electric dryers under 800?

Investing in an electric dryer under 800 is a very wise decision for many modern homeowners and renters. While high-end luxury appliances can cost thousands, the current market for budget-friendly dryers has improved significantly in terms of technology and efficiency. You are no longer sacrificing safety or performance just because you are spending less. In fact, many models in this price range include moisture sensors and digital displays that were once exclusive to premium units.

## What Is the Best Way to Choose electric dryers under 800?

The best way to choose your new dryer is to follow a systematic approach. Start by measuring the physical space where you intend to put the machine, including the path needed for the exhaust vent. Next, verify your electrical supply; this is the most common mistake buyers make. Do not buy a 240V machine if you only have a 110V outlet.

Professional tips suggest looking at the weight capacity in 'wet lbs' rather than just volume. A 1.5 cu.ft. dryer is great for about 5-10 lbs of wet clothes. Common mistakes include overloading the machine, which leads to longer drying times and motor strain. Always leave at least 25% of the drum empty to allow for proper airflow.

If you have the option, choose a model with a stainless steel tub. While plastic or coated metal is cheaper, stainless steel is much more resistant to the zippers and buttons that can scratch other surfaces. Finally, check if the venting kit is included in the price or if you need to purchase an external vent or an indoor lint trap separately. This can save you an unexpected trip to the hardware store on delivery day.
