Before you purchase, consider where you will be cleaning most. If your pet sheds mainly on a microfiber couch, a motorized brush tool is essential. However, if you are cleaning car seats or stairs, you might prioritize a lightweight design or a model with a flexible hose. Ensure the attachments match your home’s specific upholstery types.

### 2. Budget - Setting realistic price expectations
Handheld pet vacuums typically range from $40 to $150. Entry-level models around $50 usually feature Ni-MH batteries and standard brush heads. The $70-$100 range introduces lithium-ion batteries and motorized tools. Premium models above $100 offer massive suction (20,000 PA+) and larger bin capacities.

### 3. Key Features - Most important technical specifications
Look for suction power measured in PA or Volts. A good pet vacuum should have at least 14V or 10,000 PA. Battery runtime is also critical; look for at least 15-20 minutes of continuous use. Filtration is the final key feature; HEPA filters are best for trapping allergens and pet dander.

### 4. Quality & Durability - What to look for in construction
Check for models with pure-copper motors as they tend to last longer and dissipate heat better. The plastic used for the bin should be thick and impact-resistant. Brands that offer replacement parts like filters and brushes are generally a safer long-term investment.

### 5. Brand Reputation - Importance of established manufacturers
While newer brands offer high suction for less money, established names like Bissell, Shark, and BLACK+DECKER have proven track records and reliable customer service. They often donate to pet charities, which is an added bonus for animal lovers.

### 6. Portability/Size - Considerations for space and storage
A heavy vacuum will make your wrist tired during long cleaning sessions. If you have mobility issues, look for models under 2 lbs. Also, check if the vacuum comes with a wall mount or if it can stand on its own for convenient storage in small apartments.
