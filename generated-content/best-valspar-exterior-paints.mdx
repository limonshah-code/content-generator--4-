## What to Look for Before Buying the Best Valspar Exterior Paints

### Find Your Need - Compatibility and use case considerations
Before you purchase any paint, you must identify the primary material you are coating. Wood, masonry, metal, and stucco all have different porosity and expansion rates. If you are painting a porch, you need a formula designed specifically for horizontal foot traffic. For vertical siding, a formula with high vertical cling and one coat coverage is often preferred. Always check the label to ensure the paint is compatible with your specific substrate.

### Budget - Setting realistic price expectations ($X-$Y ranges)
Exterior paint generally ranges from $30 to $70 per gallon depending on the technology included. Bulk 5-gallon buckets often provide a cost savings of 10% to 20% compared to buying individual gallons. While it may be tempting to go with the cheapest option, investing in a premium paint like the Pro-Hide Gold series can actually save you money by requiring fewer coats and lasting several years longer before needing a refresh.

### Key Features - Most important technical specifications
Look for specific technological benefits like Cool Surface Technology if you are painting a pool deck, or low-temperature application features if you live in a colder climate. The finish or sheen is also a technical choice; flat finishes hide imperfections but are harder to clean, while satin and semi-gloss finishes offer better moisture resistance and are easier to wipe down but show more surface flaws.

### Quality & Durability - What to look for in construction
High-quality exterior paints are defined by their resin and pigment content. ## What Is the Best Way to Choose valspar exterior paints?

The best way to choose the perfect paint is to follow a systematic approach that starts with your environment.
1. **Analyze your climate:** If you live in a rainy area, prioritize moisture resistance and mildewcides. If you are in a sunny region, look for high UV protection.
2. **Determine the sheen:** Use flat or matte for old siding with many imperfections. Use satin or semi-gloss for trim, doors, and areas that need frequent cleaning.
3. **Test your colors:** Never buy a large quantity without testing a small sample on your actual siding. Natural light changes significantly throughout the day, and a color that looks great in the store might look very different on your house at sunset.
4. **Calculate your square footage:** Avoid the mistake of running out of paint mid-project. Measure your walls and subtract the area of windows and doors.
5. **Consider the season:** If you are painting in the fall, make sure to choose a cold-weather formula like Pro-Hide Gold that can handle dropping temperatures during the drying phase.
6. **Don't skip the primer:** Unless you are using a high-end paint-and-primer-in-one on a previously painted surface in good condition, a dedicated primer is almost always a good idea for maximum longevity.
