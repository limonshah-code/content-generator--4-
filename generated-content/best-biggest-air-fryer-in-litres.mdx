## What to Look for Before Buying the Best Biggest Air Fryer In Litres

### 1. Find Your Need - Compatibility and use case considerations
Before you commit to a purchase, you should consider exactly how many people you will be cooking for on a daily basis. If you have a large family of 6 or more, you should aim for units with at least 15 to 26 litres of capacity. For smaller families who just want to meal prep, a 6 to 10-litre unit might be sufficient. Think about the types of food you cook most; if you love rotisserie chicken, ensure the unit comes with a spit and enough internal clearance to spin a bird.

### 2. Budget - Setting realistic price expectations
Large capacity air fryers vary wildly in price. You can find basic but large basket-style fryers in the $80 to $120 range. However, if you want premium features like French doors, dual-zone baskets, or high-end stainless steel finishes, expect to pay between $150 and $300. We recommend investing a bit more in a well-known brand if you plan to use the appliance daily, as the warranty and build quality will be significantly better.

### 3. Key Features - Most important technical specifications
Look for a high wattage (1700W or higher) to ensure the unit heats up quickly and maintains temperature. Digital touchscreens are generally easier to clean than dials and offer more precise timing.
