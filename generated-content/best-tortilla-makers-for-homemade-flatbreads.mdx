Before you make a purchase, decide whether you want an electric maker or a manual press. Electric models are fantastic because they flatten and cook the dough in one step, saving you time and stove space. However, they are generally better suited for flour-based doughs. If you are a purist who loves authentic corn tortillas, a heavy manual cast iron press is often the better choice as it provides the weight needed for that perfect, thin texture.

### Budget - Setting realistic price expectations
You can find quality options in various price ranges. A basic manual aluminum press might cost between $25 and $40, while heavy-duty cast iron models usually range from $40 to $60. Electric makers with adjustable temperature controls typically fall between $50 and $90. If you are looking for a complete kit that includes warmers and specialized features, expect to spend closer to $100.

### Key Features - Most important technical specifications
Look for a diameter that matches your favorite recipes; 8 inches is standard, while 10 or 12 inches is better for burritos. For electric models, wattage is key—aim for at least 1000W to 1200W for fast heating. For manual presses, the weight of the plates and the length of the handle are the most important specs, as they determine how much effort you will need to apply.

### Quality & Durability - What to look for in construction
Cast iron is the gold standard for durability; it can literally last for generations if cared for properly. If choosing an electric model, look for a stainless steel exterior and high-quality non-stick plates. Avoid flimsy plastic handles or lightweight aluminum hinges that might snap under the pressure of a tough dough.

### Brand Reputation - Importance of established manufacturers
Brands like Victoria and Uno Casa have long histories of producing reliable cast iron kitchen tools. For electric models, brands like MasterChef and StarBlue are well-regarded for their heating consistency. Choosing a reputable brand often means you get better customer support and access to recipe guides or replacement parts.

### Portability/Size - Considerations for space and storage
Kitchen space is a premium for many people. Consider models that offer upright storage or detachable handles if you have limited cabinet space. Lighter aluminum presses are easier to move, but they might require more physical force to use compared to their heavier cast iron counterparts.
