## What to Look for Before Buying the Best Faucet Water Filter For Lead

### Find Your Need - Compatibility and use case considerations
The first step is checking your faucet type. Most faucet-mounted filters only work with standard faucets that have a removable aerator. If you have a pull-out or spray-style faucet, you will likely need an under-sink system or a dedicated beverage faucet. Consider whether you need a high-capacity unit for a large family or a small, portable one for a rental apartment.

### Budget - Setting realistic price expectations ($20-$150 ranges)
Faucet filters generally fall into three price brackets. Budget-friendly faucet mounts typically range from $20 to $40. Mid-range systems, including some higher-end horizontal mounts or basic under-sink kits, cost between $50 and $90. Premium solutions, such as dedicated solid brass faucets or complex multi-stage under-sink systems, can range from $100 to over $150. Remember to factor in the ongoing cost of replacement filters.

### Key Features - Most important technical specifications
Look for NSF/ANSI 53 certification, which specifically covers lead reduction. NSF 42 is for taste and odor (chlorine), but 53 is the gold standard for health-related contaminants. A filter life indicator (either a light or a gauge) is a vital feature to ensure you are not drinking water from an expired filter. Also, check the flow rate; a higher GPM (gallons per minute) means you won't be waiting as long to fill a glass.

### Quality & Durability - What to look for in construction
While many faucet mounts are made of plastic, look for high-impact, BPA-free materials. If you choose an under-sink system, look for stainless steel or solid brass components. The quality of the adapters is also crucial; metal adapters are much more durable and less prone to leaking than plastic ones. A sturdy switching lever for filtered vs. unfiltered water is another sign of good construction.

### Brand Reputation - Importance of established manufacturers
In the world of water filtration, certifications and brand history matter. Companies like PUR and Brita have decades of research and testing backing their products. Established brands are also more likely to have replacement filters readily available at local stores.
